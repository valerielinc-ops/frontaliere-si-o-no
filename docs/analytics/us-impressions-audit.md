# Audit — 41k USA impressions anomaly

> Task: W3-USA-AUDIT from `docs/seo-action-plan-apr20-parallel.md`.
> Date: 2026-04-20. Data period: 2026-03-23 → 2026-04-19 (28 days, GSC final).
> Source: `mcp__search-console-mcp` (Google Search Console).

## TL;DR

**Classification: mostly (a) bot/scraper noise + (d) long-tail anonymized impressions, with minor accidental ranking on EN/DE diesel-price pages.**

The United States generates 15.6% of total impressions (41,504 / 266k) but only 0.27% of clicks (24 / 8,849), for a CTR of **0.058%** — two orders of magnitude below every other country. The signature is consistent with automated query volume (recruiter-sourcing boolean strings, desktop-only, zero click-through) rather than real human expats.

**Recommended action: DO NOTHING on code. No hreflang, no geo-targeting, no noindex.** Document the pattern and monitor. Blocking or de-ranking US impressions would damage the legitimate EN-locale long-tail (expats, Ticinese diaspora, ChatGPT/Perplexity citations) with zero upside.

One low-risk, low-reward optional: the `/en/cross-border-articles/diesel-price-increase-switzerland-2026/` page is the single biggest US impression attractor (239 imp, 0 clicks, pos 6.2) and already has a scheduled rewrite in W2-DIESEL — no dedicated action needed here.

## Classification

### Primary cause: (a) Bot/scraper noise + (d) anonymized long-tail

**Evidence weight:**
1. Desktop-only concentration (98.6%), with desktop CTR 57× lower than mobile → automated.
2. 95.7% of impressions are below GSC's anonymization threshold → extreme long-tail, characteristic of query-set scrapers.
3. Visible top queries contain 26 explicit Boolean sourcing strings (recruiter syntax).
4. Steady day-over-day growth with no human traffic pattern.
5. Position is *good* (~6.0, same as human markets) — Google ranks the pages fine; the impressions just never convert.

### Secondary cause: (c) accidental ranking on fuel-price / stats / EU-agreement EN & DE pages

A minority of the US impression volume (~1,800 visible impressions across 533 queries) represents genuine Google surfacing EN/DE/FR pages to US-based users searching for Swiss-topic content ("switzerland transit fee 2026", "switzerland eu membership status 2026", "boarding schools ticino"). Some of this is probably:
- US-based Swiss expats researching.
- Academic/press users.
- AI-assistant traffic (ChatGPT Search, Perplexity) doing reference lookups — consistent with the PostHog signal documented in the main audit (ChatGPT 109 sessions / Gemini 57 sessions in 28 days).

This overlaps with **W3-AEO** (the AI SEO task). There is no reason to dampen it.

### NOT the cause

- Not a hreflang problem: `index.html` already sets correct `hreflang="it/en/de/fr"` + `x-default` at lines 56–60, and `services/seoService.ts` rewrites them per route (lines 2432–2449). Googlebot is not confused about our target locales; USA-based users are simply served EN by default, which is correct.
- Not a www/non-www split causing doubled impressions (that is W1-CANONICAL and only accounts for 45 visible US imp — negligible).
- Not a thin-content or cloaking issue: pages ranking in the US are the same pages ranking in CHE/ITA with normal CTR there.

## Recommended action: **DO NOTHING (code), monitor (ops)**

### Do not do

- **Do not add `<meta name="geo.region" content="CH-TI">`** — this tag is cargo-culted SEO; Google documented in 2019 that it ignores geo.region/geo.position. It would not fix the US impressions and clutters the `<head>`.
- **Do not add country-targeting or `noindex` for US traffic.** We cannot reliably identify "bot vs human" at request time, and GitHub Pages does not support edge logic anyway. Any static block would also de-index the legitimate 1–5% of US traffic (expats, AI crawlers, press).
- **Do not tighten hreflang further.** The current hreflang is correct. Adding `hreflang="en-CH"` or `en-GB` would not help: the non-click impressions do not arise from locale misrouting, they arise from automated query matching.
- **Do not remove EN locale pages** — EN is a documented strategic market (CHE expats, DE-speaking users who prefer EN results, AI citations).

### Do

- **Accept the pattern.** GSC is reporting truthfully, and this pattern (high-impression / zero-click USA desktop traffic on European-topic sites) is extremely common and well-documented.
- **Treat US impressions as noise when computing CTR benchmarks.** Our "real" site CTR is better calculated as `clicks / (impressions − US_desktop_impressions)` ≈ 8,825 / 225,508 ≈ **3.91%** (vs the 3.32% headline figure). Worth updating the revenue monitor.
- **Optional, low effort (covered by existing tasks):**
  - W2-DIESEL already rewrites title/meta on the top US page (`/en/cross-border-articles/diesel-price-increase-switzerland-2026/`) — if real US expats are a fraction of the 239 imp, better snippets will pick up incremental clicks for free.
  - W3-AEO will document AI-bot citations; if ChatGPT/Perplexity referrals grow, part of this US impression block may re-classify as legitimate AEO upside.
- **Monitor via `scripts/revenue-monitor.mjs` (W3-MONITORING).** Add an alert: if USA impressions exceed 20% of total *and* USA CTR remains < 0.2%, log noise metric; if USA CTR ever crosses 1%, re-audit (it would mean either real traffic emerged or the bot stopped).

### Why not block the obvious bot queries via robots.txt?

`robots.txt` applies to crawler paths, not SERP query strings. We cannot tell Google "do not show us for query X". We could hypothetically serve `User-Agent`-based 403s, but:
1. GitHub Pages does not let us.
2. The scrapers use the Google SERP API, not direct site crawls — they never request our URLs, so blocking them is architecturally impossible.
3. These impressions do not consume our crawl budget or server resources; they are a reporting nuisance, not a cost.

## Expected outcome

- **No change to code.** No change to hreflang, no change to meta tags, no change to robots.txt.
- **Doc-only PR** published at `docs/analytics/us-impressions-audit.md`.
- **Follow-up** (separate task, covered by W3-MONITORING): extend revenue-monitor to compute and track a "noise-adjusted CTR" metric.
- **Re-review in 90 days.** If USA impressions keep growing at the current linear rate (~60 imp/day), re-evaluate; if they plateau or drop, no action.
